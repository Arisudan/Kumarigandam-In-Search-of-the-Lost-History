# Chapter 19: The Silence Below

The entrance closed behind them, sealing the world above. Darkness took over, and only the faint beams of their lights cut through the path ahead. They began their descent—slow, careful, step by step—into the unknown.

The passage was narrower than before. The walls were plain: no carvings, no symbols, nothing. 

“That’s strange,” Sharan whispered. “No markings at all…”

Abinaya held the scroll tightly. “This place wasn’t meant to guide…” she said softly, “…it was meant to hide.”

Joseph walked ahead, alert and focused. The air grew heavier and thicker; each breath felt slightly harder. Keerthi slowed down. “Do you feel that?”

Harish nodded. “It’s not just the air…” Something else was there. A pressure—not on their bodies, but inside their minds.

They continued walking. Then, it began. A faint whisper. Sharan stopped. “Did you hear that?”

Silence. Then again—whispers. Broken. Unclear. Keerthi turned around quickly. “There’s no one…”

Harish held his head slightly. “It’s not outside…” he said slowly, “…it’s inside us.”

Images flashed—memories and fears. Abinaya saw the moment she slipped, falling again and again. She closed her eyes. “No… this is not real.”

Joseph spoke firmly. “Focus. Don’t let it control you.”

They moved forward together, stronger. But the whispers followed until Joseph suddenly raised his hand. “Stop.”

Everyone froze. Something was ahead—a shape on the ground. They moved closer, slowly and carefully. The light fell over it: a body, lying still and unmoving.

It wasn't bones or ancient remains. It was a human, but not recent either. The clothes were old and worn out, nearly torn apart by time, the fabric faded from another era. Beside the body lay equipment: a rusted, heavy, outdated torch and a corroded, bulky oxygen tank.

Everything spoke one truth—this man had been here for nearly a century.

Sharan whispered, “This is decades old…”

Joseph nodded. “Maybe ninety… even a hundred years.”

Keerthi stepped back slightly. “Then… who is this?”

Near the chest, a small leather notebook rested. Abinaya picked it up gently and brushed off the dust. Her fingers froze. “**R. Nedunchozhan**…”

Silence. Joseph’s voice dropped. “He was one of the earliest explorers… studied lost lands…”

Harish whispered, “He was declared lost at sea…”

Joseph nodded. “He didn’t disappear…”

They all looked at the body. “He reached here.”

Abinaya opened the notebook carefully. She read aloud: *“This is not just a ruin… It is a test… Every step is watched…”*

A chill passed through them. She turned the pages. Then, she saw a drawing of an orange gemstone—radiant and alive. She read again: *“Not Maragatham… far greater… It holds power beyond value…”*

Sharan pointed near the body. A faint glow appeared. Joseph uncovered it: the orange gemstone. It was brighter, warmer, and stronger than the green one. 

Abinaya whispered, “This is what he found…” She turned to the last page. *“Do not trust the path… Something… is waiting…”*

Silence. The warning lingered. Keerthi stepped back. “I don’t like this…”

Sharan looked ahead. “We’re following his path…”

Joseph stood up slowly, holding the gemstone. “And we know how it ended.”

Harish looked forward into the darkness. “But not for us.”

He bent down and picked up Nedunchozhan’s worn bag. Abinaya secured the notebook safely while Joseph held the orange gemstone firmly. They looked back for one last time at the body, the past, and the warning. Then they turned forward.

Behind them, the entrance was gone—sealed. There was no way back. Only one path remained: forward, into the unknown. And somewhere ahead, beyond the darkness, the truth was waiting.
