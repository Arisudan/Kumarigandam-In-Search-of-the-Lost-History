# Chapter 15: The Hidden City Beneath

The moment they stepped inside, the light from the outside disappeared. Darkness surrounded them. Only the soft glow of Joseph’s lantern and Keerthi’s torch guided their way.

The air felt cold, still, and untouched for centuries. Each step echoed softly through the narrow stone passage. Harish looked back once. The entrance behind them remained open, but the forest light felt far away now—like they had crossed into another world.

“Stay close,” Joseph said. His voice was calm but serious.

The passage sloped downward, slowly taking them deeper underground. The walls were carved with ancient markings, symbols, lines, and stories frozen in stone. Abinaya ran her fingers lightly across them. “These are not random carvings…” she whispered. “They’re records.”

Sharan quickly took out his notebook. “Document everything,” he said.

Keerthi moved her torch carefully across the walls. Some carvings showed people, some showed ships, and some showed land—large land, stretching far beyond the ocean. Harish stopped walking. “Is that…?”

Abinaya nodded slowly. “A map.”

They continued walking. The passage slowly widened and the ceiling grew higher. The air felt slightly easier to breathe. Then they reached an opening. All of them stopped, frozen. 

In front of them was something none of them expected: a vast underground space. Not just a chamber, not just ruins, but a hidden city. Stone structures stood across the wide cavern—pillars, broken rooftops, and carved streets, all preserved in silence. Dim light filtered through cracks above, just enough to reveal shapes in the distance.

Keerthi’s voice trembled. “This… is a city.”

Joseph nodded slowly. “Or what’s left of one.”

Harish walked forward carefully. Everywhere he looked, there were signs of life: buildings, platforms, and statues. This was not just a ruin; this was a place where people once lived, worked, and loved.

Abinaya slowly opened the **Kadalkol** book again. Her eyes moved across the pages, then she looked up at the city, then back at the book. “It matches…” she said softly. “This place is mentioned here.”

Sharan looked around in disbelief. “So this island…” he paused, “…is only the surface.”

Joseph completed the thought. “And this is what lies beneath.”

As they walked deeper into the underground city, they began noticing something else: statues. Everywhere. Tall, beautifully carved, and all facing one direction. “Why are they all facing the same way?” Keerthi asked.

Joseph looked ahead. “Maybe they’re pointing us somewhere.”

They followed the direction of the statues step by step, through broken pathways and across fallen stones, until finally they reached a large open square. At the center of it stood two grand statues, side by side, unbroken and untouched. 

One was of a king—strong, proud, holding a sword. The other was of a woman—graceful, calm, holding a lotus. Abinaya stepped forward slowly, her eyes filled with emotion. “Harish…” she whispered, “these must be…”

Harish nodded. “Athiyan… and Mathi.”

At the base of the statues, a stone platform stood with ancient inscriptions carved across it. Abinaya knelt down and read softly. “This place… was built as the heart of the southern lands… When the seas rise… the truth must sleep beneath the earth… until the chosen return.”

A deep silence followed. Harish looked around. “This wasn’t destroyed…” he said slowly, “it was hidden.”

Joseph nodded. “Protected.”

As they moved around the statues, Keerthi suddenly called out. “Wait… there’s something here.”

Everyone turned. Behind the statues, part of the stone wall looked different—smoother and more structured. Sharan stepped closer and brushed away the dust. A small opening revealed itself. Inside it was a stone puzzle lock, circular, with movable engraved symbols: Square, Diamond, Circle, Rectangle, and Triangle.

Joseph leaned in. “A lock mechanism.”

Sharan nodded. “And not a simple one.”

Abinaya stepped forward slowly, her eyes fixed on the symbols. Something about them felt familiar. Very familiar. She suddenly froze. “I’ve seen this before.”

Harish looked at her. “Where?”

Abinaya quickly opened the **Kadalkol** book. Her fingers flipped through the pages rapidly until she stopped, pointing to a section. “These symbols…” she whispered, “they appear here… in a sequence.”

Sharan leaned closer. “What sequence?”

Abinaya read carefully. “Square… Diamond… Circle… Rectangle… Triangle.”

Joseph looked back at the puzzle lock. “The same symbols.”

Harish smiled slightly. “So it’s not random.”

Abinaya reached toward the lock, her hands steady. She began rotating the stone symbols one by one, carefully placing them in the exact order from the book: Square, Diamond, Circle, Rectangle, Triangle.

For a moment, nothing happened. Then—*click*. A deep sound echoed. The wall trembled slightly. Dust fell from above. The stone surface behind the puzzle slowly shifted. A hidden door began to open.

Cold air rushed out from inside. Darkness waited beyond—ancient, silent, and unseen for centuries. Joseph lifted the lantern slightly. “Well…” he said quietly, “looks like we’ve just unlocked the next part.”

Abinaya closed the book slowly, her eyes fixed on the opening. “This is where the real story continues.”

Harish stood beside her. “Then we go together.”

The doorway opened fully, revealing a path into deeper darkness. And whatever lay beyond it had been waiting for a very long time.
