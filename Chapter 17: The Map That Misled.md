# Chapter 17: The Map That Misled

The room was silent. All eyes were fixed on the second scroll. Abinaya slowly reached forward, her fingers hovering over it for a moment. Then, she picked it up.

No sound. No movement. No hidden mechanism. Joseph exhaled softly. “Looks like we’re safe… for now.”

Abinaya carefully unrolled the scroll. The others leaned closer, expecting—hoping—for the missing half. But the moment the scroll opened, their expressions changed.

“This… isn’t right,” Sharan said.

Harish frowned. “This doesn’t match.”

Abinaya’s eyes moved quickly across the markings. Her voice dropped. “It’s not the other half.”

Silence. The scroll did contain a map, but not the one they expected. This map was smaller, more detailed, and completely different. Keerthi looked confused. “Then what is this?”

Joseph leaned closer, his eyes sharpened. “This… is a location map.”

Sharan nodded slowly. “Not a territory map. A path.”

Abinaya traced the lines carefully. Narrow routes. Turns. Symbols marking specific points. “This leads somewhere…” she whispered.

Harish noticed something near the edge. “Look at this.” At the corner of the scroll, there it was: the royal symbol of Athiyan. Clear and undeniable.

Joseph straightened up. “So this is still connected to him.”

Abinaya nodded. “Yes… but not directly to the main map.”

Keerthi looked between both scrolls. “One shows a territory… the other shows a path…”

Sharan completed the thought. “Then this is not the destination. It’s a route.”

Harish looked back at the chamber around them. “So… this place was just a step.”

A realization settled over them. They had not found the full truth, not yet. They had only unlocked the next direction. Abinaya studied the second map again. Her expression grew serious. “This is not simple.”

The path drawn on the scroll twisted in strange ways: sharp turns, dead ends, and hidden markings. Joseph crossed his arms. “This was designed to confuse.”

Sharan nodded. “Or to protect something.”

Abinaya pointed to a specific part of the map. “Look here.” A symbol marked the final point—different from the others and more detailed.

Harish leaned closer. “What is that?”

Abinaya shook her head slowly. “I don’t know yet… but it’s important.”

Keerthi looked around the chamber again. “So where does this path start?”

Silence. All of them slowly turned, looking around the room—the table, the walls, the entrance. Joseph spoke quietly. “If this map leads somewhere underground… then the starting point should be somewhere here.”

Sharan picked up his device again. “I’ll scan the area.” The machine hummed softly, searching and detecting. They began to move around the chamber carefully and systematically.

Harish checked the walls, running his hands along the stone, looking for hidden grooves or loose sections. Keerthi moved along the edges of the floor, tapping lightly and listening for hollow spaces. Joseph examined the stone table again. “Sometimes the entry is right where you stand,” he said.

Abinaya stood still for a moment, looking at the map, then at the room, then back at the map. Her eyes narrowed slightly. “This… doesn’t match this room.”

Everyone looked at her. “What do you mean?” Harish asked.

Abinaya pointed at the beginning of the map. “The starting symbol…” she said, “it’s not here.”

Sharan walked over quickly. “You’re right.” The symbol shown at the beginning of the path was not present anywhere inside the chamber.

Joseph stepped back. “So the starting point… is not here.”

Silence. Then Harish spoke. “Then it must be somewhere outside.”

Keerthi nodded. “In the underground city.”

Abinaya folded the map slowly. “That’s why this place didn’t react.”

Joseph gave a slight smile. “This was just where we found the clue.”

Sharan looked toward the doorway. “Not where we begin.”

A new kind of excitement filled the room. Not confusion anymore, but direction. Harish looked at Abinaya. “So what do we do next?”

Abinaya held both scrolls firmly, her voice steady. “We go back.”

Joseph nodded. “And we find the starting point.”

Sharan adjusted his device. “This time… we scan the entire city.”

Keerthi smiled slightly. “Looks like the real search begins now.”

Harish took one last look around the chamber, then toward the dark path outside. *This island is guiding us… step by step.*

They moved toward the exit, leaving the chamber behind but not its secrets. Because now, they had something more powerful than answers—they had a direction. And somewhere in the underground city, hidden in plain sight, the true path was waiting to be found.
