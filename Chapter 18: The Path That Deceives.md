# Chapter 18: The Path That Deceives

The underground city stood silent once again as the five of them stepped out of the chamber. The air felt heavier now—not just with age, but with purpose.

Abinaya held the second scroll tightly. “This is where it begins,” she said.

Joseph nodded. “Let’s not rush.”

They moved slowly into the city, this time not just exploring, but searching. Sharan opened his device. “I’ll track unusual readings.” Keerthi scanned the surroundings with her torch. “I’ll watch for markings.” Harish stayed close to Abinaya. “Tell us what to look for.”

Abinaya unfolded the scroll again, her eyes fixed on the first symbol—a small marking at the beginning of the path. Simple, but unique. “A square… with a line across it,” she said.

They began their search through broken pathways, across fallen pillars, and between silent buildings. Minutes passed, then more. Sharan suddenly stopped. “Wait.” He pointed toward a stone pillar. Carved faintly, almost hidden, was the same symbol. “The starting mark,” he said.

Everyone gathered, hope rising. “This must be it,” Keerthi said.

Joseph looked around carefully. “Too easy.”

But still, they decided to follow it. The path led them through a narrow passage, walls closing in, air tightening. The symbols continued, one after another, leading them deeper. Harish whispered, “This feels right.”

Abinaya nodded. “Yes…” But something in her eyes was uncertain.

They reached a small chamber, closed and sealed. At its center was a stone plate. Joseph stepped forward. “This looks like an entrance.” Sharan checked his device. “Strong readings here.” Keerthi smiled slightly. “We found it.”

Abinaya hesitated. Her eyes moved back to the scroll, then to the symbol on the wall, then back again. Something didn’t match. “Wait,” she said softly.

But before she could finish, Joseph stepped onto the stone plate. *Click.*

The ground trembled. “Joseph!” Harish shouted.

The walls shifted suddenly. From both sides, stone panels slid inward. “A trap!” Sharan yelled. Joseph jumped back just in time as the stone panels crashed shut where he had stood seconds ago. Dust filled the air. Everyone stepped back quickly, breathing fast. 

Silence. Then, before they could recover, another sound echoed: *Crack.*

The ground beneath Abinaya’s feet gave way. “Abinaya!” Harish shouted. She slipped, falling into a narrow pit that opened suddenly beneath her. Her hand caught the edge, barely holding on. “Help!” she gasped.

Without thinking, Harish rushed forward and dropped to the ground, grabbing her hand tightly. “Hold on!” he shouted. The ground around them trembled slightly, dust falling and the pit shifting. Abinaya struggled, her grip slipping. “Harish… I can’t—”

“You can!” he said, his voice breaking. “Don’t let go!”

For a moment, time slowed. Harish’s eyes filled with tears—not fear or panic, but something deeper. A thought hit him, sharp and painful: *What if she falls… what if I lose her…*

His grip tightened. “I’m not letting you go!” he shouted. With all his strength, he pulled. Sharan and Joseph rushed forward to help. Together, they lifted her up. 

Abinaya collapsed onto the ground, breathing heavily. Harish didn’t move; he just looked at her, his eyes still wet. For a second, he said nothing. Then suddenly, he pulled her into a tight embrace, holding her close, firm and unshaken.

“I thought…” he whispered. “I thought I lost you…”

Abinaya closed her eyes, holding him back just as tightly. “I’m here…”

No more words were needed—just silence and understanding. It was a moment that said everything they had never spoken. A few steps away, Sharan looked at Keerthi with a small smile, eyes slightly wet. Joseph looked away quietly, letting them have that moment.

After a few seconds, Harish slowly let go, but not completely. “You okay?” he asked softly. Abinaya nodded, a gentle smile forming. “Yes.”

They stood up, stronger and closer. The trap lay behind them now—a warning and a lesson. Abinaya looked at the scroll again, her voice steady now. “This is wrong.”

Joseph nodded. “No more assumptions.”

They stepped away from the false chamber, more careful and more aware. This time, they observed everything. Abinaya studied the scroll deeply—every line, every turn. Then she stopped. “The starting point…” she whispered, “…is not on a wall.”

Harish looked around. “Then where?”

Abinaya turned slowly, her eyes scanning the ground, the structures, and the layout. Then she pointed. “The statues.”

They moved back toward the central square. This time, they looked differently. Sharan circled the statues. “Look at their direction…” Keerthi followed. “They’re pointing somewhere.”

Abinaya aligned the map step by step. Then she saw it: a faint marking on the ground. The real starting symbol. “This is it,” she said.

Sharan checked his device. “Strong signal… below.”

They cleared the surface, and a hidden circular entrance revealed itself. Joseph smiled slightly. “Now this… feels right.”

Harish looked at Abinaya. She nodded—no fear, only trust. Together, they stood before the real path. And this time, they were ready.
