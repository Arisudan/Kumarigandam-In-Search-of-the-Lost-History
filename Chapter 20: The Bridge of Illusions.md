# Chapter 20: The Bridge of Illusions

They walked forward, leaving behind the body of Nedunchozhan, his warning, and the truth he had uncovered. Joseph carried the orange gemstone carefully, Harish held the worn bag, and Abinaya secured the notebook. No one spoke, because now they knew: this place was not just ancient—it was watching.

The path ahead stretched into darkness. Step by step, they moved forward with caution. Then suddenly, the path opened wide.

All of them stopped. In front of them was a bridge—long, narrow, and suspended across a massive chamber. But what lay beneath it was unbelievable.

Gold. Mountains of gold. Coins, jewels, crowns, and ancient ornaments. A treasure beyond imagination, the light shimmering and reflected, almost alive.

Keerthi whispered, “This… this is unreal…”

Sharan stepped closer, eyes wide. “This is beyond anything we’ve seen…”

Harish frowned. Something felt wrong. It was too perfect, too inviting, and too easy. At that moment, Keerthi quickly opened Nedunchozhan’s notebook, turning the remaining pages. Her eyes scanned quickly, and then she froze.

“Wait.” Everyone turned toward her. Her voice became firm. “This is not real.”

Silence. Joseph stepped closer. “What do you mean?”

Keerthi read aloud: *“The treasure beneath the bridge is not gold… It is an illusion… A trap designed for the greedy… Those who reach for it… never return…”*

A chill spread through the group. Harish stepped back slightly. “So… it’s a trap.”

Sharan nodded slowly. “A psychological one.”

Joseph looked at the gold again. Now, it didn’t look beautiful; it looked dangerous. 

Abinaya spoke calmly. “Don’t look down.”

Harish nodded. “Focus only on the path.”

At the same time, Abinaya opened the reference book she had taken from the village temple library. Her fingers moved quickly across the pages, searching, matching, and understanding. Her eyes sharpened. “The symbols match…” she said.

Sharan leaned closer. “You figured it out?”

Abinaya nodded. “Yes. This book explains path structures and directional markings.” She pointed ahead. “The map becomes clearer now.”

Joseph gave a small smile. “Good.”

Abinaya looked at all of them. “We walk straight. No distractions. No stopping.”

One by one, they stepped onto the bridge. Carefully. The illusion shimmered below; the gold sparkled brighter, almost calling them, trying to pull their eyes down. Keerthi clenched her fists, resisting. Sharan kept his gaze forward. Joseph walked steady and unshaken. Harish stayed close to Abinaya, focused only on her steps.

Step by step by step. The bridge felt endless, and the whispers tried to return, but they ignored everything until finally, they reached the other side. Safe.

All of them stepped down, breathing deeply. Sharan let out a small laugh. “That… was close.”

Keerthi smiled softly. “We didn’t fall for it.”

Joseph nodded. “Knowledge saved us.”

Harish looked at Abinaya. “And trust.”

They turned forward. And then, they saw it—a massive chamber wall, towering, sealed, and ancient. At its center was a carved slot.

Abinaya stepped closer. “This must be the next gate.”

Joseph took out the Maragatham gemstone. “This worked before…” He placed it into the slot. Nothing. No sound. No movement.

Sharan frowned. “That’s strange…”

Keerthi stepped closer. “Try the orange one.”

Joseph nodded and carefully placed the orange gemstone. Again—nothing. Silence. A heavy silence.

Abinaya opened her reference book again, flipping pages quickly. “These carvings…” she said slowly, “they don’t mention gemstones.”

Sharan looked confused. “Then what is this slot for?”

Joseph stepped back slightly, thinking. “Maybe we’re overthinking…”

Keerthi bent closer, observing carefully. “It’s not deep…” she said.

Everyone leaned in. The slot was flat and shallow, not shaped for a gemstone. Harish, who had been silent, stepped forward. He crouched down, examining it closely. His fingers traced the edges, his eyes narrowed.

“This isn’t for a stone…” he said quietly. They all looked at him. “It’s too shallow… too precise… it’s meant for something flat.”

Silence. “A flat object…” Sharan repeated.

Harish looked up. “Like a coin.”

Joseph froze. His eyes widened. “The coin…”

Keerthi gasped. “The one from the island!”

Sharan immediately turned. Joseph quickly took it out—the Chola gold coin, old and perfectly shaped. He handed it to Sharan. “Try it.”

Sharan stepped forward slowly and carefully. He placed the coin into the slot. It slid in perfectly. For a moment, nothing happened. Then—*click*.

A deep mechanical sound echoed. The carvings began to shift. The chamber trembled. The massive door started unlocking. The coin was pulled inside, disappearing into the mechanism.

And then, beside the slot, a new opening appeared—a narrow hole, just enough for a hand. Everyone stepped back slightly.

“What now…” Keerthi whispered.

Joseph stared at it. “This is not over.”

Harish stepped forward slowly, his eyes fixed on the opening. The door was unlocked, but not open. Waiting. For something more. For someone. 

And whatever lay beyond was closer than ever.
