# Chapter 14: The Forest That Remembers

Morning came slowly. The first light of the sun touched the ocean, spreading gold across the water. Waves whispered gently against the shore. For a moment, everything felt calm.

Harish woke up first. He stepped out of the tent and looked at the island. The trees stood still. The same silence from the night remained. But in daylight, the island looked different—more alive, more real, and yet still unknown.

One by one, the others woke up. Keerthi stretched and smiled. “Feels like we’re in another world.”

Sharan nodded. “In a way… we are.”

Joseph was already near the boat, checking their equipment. “We go into the forest today,” he said firmly.

Abinaya stepped out, holding the **Kadalkol** book. “I think the answers we need… are inside this island.”

Harish looked at her. “And inside that book.”

She nodded. They quickly packed their essentials: water, tools, the detection device, and the lantern. Most importantly, they took the Maragatham gemstone and the Kadalkol book, which Abinaya secured carefully in her bag.

Within minutes, they stepped into the forest together. The path from the previous night was still visible. Sharan checked his notes. “Same direction,” he said.

Joseph led the way. The group moved cautiously; every step felt like walking through history. The deeper they went, the quieter it became. No birds. No animal sounds. Only the soft movement of leaves and their own footsteps.

Soon, they reached the stone wall again. Now, in daylight, it looked even more mysterious. The wall stretched far beyond what they could see, covered in carvings and worn by time, but still strong and still standing.

Harish ran his fingers along the surface. “These markings…”

Abinaya stepped closer. Her eyes widened. “These are similar to the ones in Kadalkol.”

She quickly opened the book, comparing symbols, lines, and patterns. “They match,” she said softly.

Joseph looked at the wall again. “This isn’t just a boundary.”

Sharan nodded. “It’s part of something bigger.”

Keerthi walked slowly along the wall. “Wait…” she called out. “There’s something here.”

Everyone moved toward her. Part of the wall looked different. The stones were arranged in a slightly curved shape, almost like a sealed entrance. Joseph raised the lantern, even in daylight. “This looks like a gate.”

Harish stepped closer. “But it’s locked… or buried.”

Abinaya gently touched the surface. At that exact moment, the Maragatham gemstone inside her bag began to glow faintly. She froze. “Did you see that?”

Harish looked at her bag. “The gemstone…”

Abinaya slowly took it out. The green light shimmered softly—reacting, responding to the wall. 

Sharan stepped back slightly. “This is not a coincidence.”

Joseph crossed his arms. “This wall is connected to the gemstone.”

Abinaya’s heart began to race. “This must be another mechanism… like the temple.” She looked at the stone surface again, then at the gemstone, then back at the carvings. Her mind began connecting the clues. “The shape…” she whispered. “There must be a slot… somewhere here.”

They began searching the wall carefully, running their hands across every stone, checking for gaps, edges, and patterns. Minutes passed.

“Here!” Keerthi called.

A small circular indentation was hidden beneath layers of sand and moss. It was almost invisible but perfectly shaped. Harish cleared the surface. The slot revealed itself clearly—exactly the size of the Maragatham gemstone.

Silence fell, the same kind of silence they had felt in the underwater temple. Joseph looked at Abinaya. “Looks like we found another door.”

Abinaya held the gemstone tightly. Her hands trembled slightly. Harish noticed. “It’s okay,” he said softly. “We’re together.”

She nodded, took a deep breath, and slowly moved the gemstone toward the slot. For a moment, time felt still. Then she placed it inside.

A deep sound echoed through the ground. The wall trembled. Dust fell from the stones. Ancient mechanisms hidden for centuries began to move. The earth itself seemed to awaken.

Sharan stepped back. “It’s opening!”

The stone wall slowly split apart. A narrow entrance revealed itself—dark, silent, and ancient. Air rushed out from inside, cold and untouched. 

Joseph looked into the darkness. “Well…” He smiled slightly. “Looks like we just found the real entrance.”

Harish looked at Abinaya. “This is it.”

Abinaya nodded. “The next part of the story.”

They stood at the edge of the opening. The island had shown them its first secret, but this was something deeper, something hidden for centuries. 

Joseph lifted the lantern. “Stay close. No rushing.”

Sharan adjusted his device. Keerthi held onto her torch. Harish stood beside Abinaya. Ready. Together, they stepped inside. 

And behind them, the forest stood silent once again, as if it had been waiting for this moment.
